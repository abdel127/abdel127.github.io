Animation in Processing

Some of the animation projects that I have worked on in Processing. Displayed are particle simulations as well as fluid motion systems.

Fluid Cloth Simulation:

A mass-spring system (with nodes and vertices) was used to create a realistic moving cloth. 3 spheres are spawned to physically collide with each other as well as the cloth. One of the spheres is controlled by the user in order to interact with the cloth. The cloth is 30x30 nodes big. The camera can be controlled to rotate and zoom.

Features:

1. 3D Mass-spring cloth simulation
2. 3D Rendering with user controlled camera
3. Two-way Coupling object simulation
4. 3D sphere-sphere interactions

Firework Particle Generator:

A user-controlled firework generator. Once left click is pressed, a rocket-like obstacle that flies up to the clicked location then explodes with audio. The particles change color as they expand away from the explosion point. The fireworks also explode into random colors! A firework sound gets emitted with each click of the mouse. The camera can be controlled to rotate and zoom. Sound library used.

Demo Video:

Water Fountain Particles:

A demo of a water fountain. Particles spawn from the center of the fountain with regular velocities. They are affected by gravity and lose color as they travel. The camera can be controlled to rotate and zoom in respects to the water fountain. PeasyCam library used.

Demo Video:

Fire Particle Simulation:

The individual particles of fire are textured with an image of fire, and they also get translucent the longer they are alive. Above the fire, there is smoke, which adds to the realistic feel of the simulation. The user gets control of the camera in order to explore the 3-D scene. PeasyCam library used.

Demo Video:

Bouncing Ball:

My first visual project. A textured ball spawns and bounces in an enclosed room. Clicking the left mouse button will apply a random force to the ball, allowing it to keep moving. A realistic shadow was created under the ball and follows its location.
